# Markets with high potential for VoiceItNow

VoiceItNow.org is a **free iOS app** that allows survivors of domestic abuse to securely record voice journals (with AI‑generated transcripts), collect media evidence and build a legal timeline of abuse.  It includes emergency tools, allows evidence to be stored off‑device and offers a shelter/non‑profit locator【85166148072954†L49-L130】.  Since VoiceItNow is iOS‑only, the best markets combine **high domestic‑violence prevalence** with **large iPhone adoption** and **user segments who need discreet evidence‑collection tools**.

## 4 Marketing strategies for sensitive audiences

1. **Discreet and trauma‑informed branding.**  Survivors may hide their app usage from abusers.  Consider using a neutral app icon and name, similar to how *EasyRescue* disguised itself and used women‑only marketing channels【288324796355696†L149-L267】.  Provide quick‑exit functions to hide evidence in emergencies.

2. **Partner channels over broad advertising.**  Survivors often seek help through trusted organizations.  Collaborate with shelters, law enforcement, legal‑aid clinics, healthcare providers and Indigenous community centers to distribute the app and build trust.  Provide staff with training and promotional materials.

3. **Leverage Apple’s ecosystem.**  With high iPhone adoption in North America, highlight Apple‑specific advantages (e.g., secure on‑device encryption, iCloud backup, Siri shortcuts).  Explore integration with the **Apple Watch** and **CarPlay** to allow hands‑free emergency actions.

4. **Targeted digital outreach.**  Use search‑engine ads and social‑media campaigns focused on queries like “document abuse,” “covert recording,” or “legal evidence app.”  To protect users, avoid explicit domestic‑violence terms in the ad copy and instead emphasize “voice diary,” “safe journaling” or “personal evidence organiser.”

5. **Multilingual support.**  Expand beyond English to Spanish, French and Indigenous languages.  Many survivors in the U.S. and Canada are immigrants or members of Indigenous communities who may not speak English as a first language.

6. **Education and empowerment.**  Provide in‑app content on safety planning, recognizing abuse, digital privacy and legal rights.  Link users to online courses and helplines.  Offer shareable resources that friends and family can use to support survivors.

## Conclusion

The best markets for launching **VoiceItNow** combine **high domestic‑violence prevalence**, **strong iPhone adoption** and **demographics that value discrete, secure evidence‑collection tools**.  North America—particularly the United States and Canada—meets these conditions: domestic abuse is widespread, iOS accounts for roughly **60 % of mobile devices**【995334923232862†L18-L25】【768148744650617†L18-L23】, and the women‑safety‑app market is growing rapidly【771899644430487†L115-L125】.  Early marketing should focus on **young adults**, **women of colour**, **Indigenous communities**, **LGBTQ+ individuals** and **people with disabilities**, and should leverage partnerships with shelters, legal‑aid organizations, health providers and universities.  Scandinavia (Norway and Sweden) and other high‑income regions with large iPhone markets offer promising international opportunities once the app gains traction in North America.  By combining voice‑based evidence collection, trauma‑informed design and strategic partnerships, VoiceItNow can fill a critical gap for survivors who need to document abuse safely and seek justice.


# Markets with high potential for VoiceItNow

**VoiceItNow.org** is a free iOS app that allows survivors of domestic abuse to securely record voice journals (with AI‑generated transcripts), collect media evidence, and build a legal timeline of abuse.

It includes:
- Secure voice and media evidence collection  
- AI-generated transcripts  
- Off-device evidence storage  
- Emergency tools  
- A shelter and non-profit locator  

Learn more: https://voiceitnow.org

Because VoiceItNow is **iOS-only**, the strongest markets combine:
- **High domestic-violence prevalence**
- **High iPhone adoption**
- **User segments that require discreet, private evidence-collection tools**

---

## North America Focus

### United States

**Why it matters**
- Approximately **1 in 3 women** and **1 in 4 men** in the U.S. experience physical violence by an intimate partner.
- Around **10 million people per year** experience intimate partner violence.
- Black women experience domestic violence at rates approximately **35% higher** than White women.
- More than **1 in 3 Indigenous women** experience domestic violence in their lifetime.
- Young adults (18–24) experience the highest first exposure rates to intimate partner violence.

**Why VoiceItNow fits**
- iOS market share in the U.S. is ~**59%** (StatCounter, 2025)
- Survivors often do not report abuse due to fear, retaliation, or lack of evidence
- Voice-based journaling enables fast, low-friction documentation immediately after incidents

Useful references:
- https://www.cdc.gov/violenceprevention/intimatepartnerviolence
- https://www.lifewire.org/community-advocacy/dv-by-the-numbers
- https://gs.statcounter.com/os-market-share/mobile/united-states-of-america

### Canada

**Why it matters**
- **44% of women** in Canada report experiencing psychological, physical, or sexual abuse by an intimate partner in their lifetime.
- Indigenous and LGBTQ+ populations experience significantly higher rates.
- Most incidents are never reported to police.

**Why VoiceItNow fits**
- iOS market share in Canada is ~**61%**
- Strong nonprofit, healthcare, and shelter networks for distribution

References:
- https://www.canada.ca/en/women-gender-equality/gender-based-violence/intimate-partner-violence.html
- https://gs.statcounter.com/os-market-share/mobile/canada

## High-Value User Segments (Cross-Market)

- **College students & young adults** (18–34)
- **LGBTQ+ individuals**
- **Indigenous communities**
- **Immigrant & undocumented survivors**
- **Women with disabilities**
- **Survivors navigating custody or protective-order cases**

These groups disproportionately:
- Experience abuse
- Face credibility challenges
- Need timestamped, structured, private documentation

---

## Distribution Channels That Convert

- Domestic violence shelters & advocacy orgs
- Campus Title IX offices and counseling centers
- Family law attorneys and legal aid clinics
- Emergency departments & trauma-informed healthcare providers
- Online survivor communities and crisis-resource directories

---

## Key Takeaway

VoiceItNow is best positioned in **English-speaking, high‑iOS‑adoption countries** with strong domestic-violence advocacy ecosystems—starting with the **United States and Canada**—and should focus outreach on **young adults, marginalized communities, and institutions that support survivors**.
